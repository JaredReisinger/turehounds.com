For full transparency, here’s what our puppy agreement looks like, with some annotations explaining why certain clauses are included. Note that the majority of the “burden” from the agreement is on us, not on the new puppy home.

Ture Hounds

COMPANION DOG AGREEMENT

This contract is made this Nth day of MONTH, YEAR, between Barbara & Jared Reisinger of ADDRESS, Tel: TELEPHONE, hereinafter termed the SELLER; and NEW OWNER NAMES of NEW OWNER ADDRESS, Tel: NEW OWNER TELEPHONE, hereinafter termed the BUYER.
Standard contract info, listing the date and the people involved.
The SELLER agrees to sell, and hereby does sell, to the BUYER an AKC-registerable COLOR Basenji MALE/FEMALE, whelped DATE OF BIRTH, hereinafter termed the PUPPY. The PUPPY’s designated AKC registration number is REGISTRATION NUMBER; she is sired by THE FATHER, AKC registration REGISTRATION NUMBER; out of THE MOTHER, AKC registration REGISTRATION NUMBER. The PUPPY’s HomeAgain microchip number is MICROCHIP NUMBER.
Uniquely identifying information about the puppy.
The consideration for this sale has three components:
  1. The sum of THE PRICE which has been received in full by the SELLER.
  2. The SELLER will be listed as a co-owner on the PUPPY until all obligations are fulfilled.
    See AKC REGISTRATION, below, for details.

NAME

The PUPPY will be AKC registered as “REGISTERED NAME”. The PUPPY’s call name (“CALL NAME”) was selected by the BUYER and that call name has been reported to and accepted by the SELLER.
Included to show what we will register as the “official” registered name of the puppy.

AKC REGISTRATION

The BUYER and SELLER agree that the AKC registration of the PUPPY will show four owners: BUYER’S NAMES, Barbara Reisinger, and Jared Reisinger, until other provisions in this agreement are met. Once met, the AKC registration will be transferred in accordance with said provisions.
A common question is “Why do you stay on as owners”? We do this as a “just in case” for things like the dog getting loose and picked up by animal control. Being listed as an owner means we can help get the dog back and returned to you.

DISPOSITION OR RELINQUISHMENT

If the BUYER is unable or unwilling to reside with the PUPPY at any time during the PUPPY’s lifetime for any reason, the PUPPY will be returned to the SELLER, and the BUYER shall be entitled to $200.00, less any medical expenses, including a standard veterinary exam, incurred by the condition of the PUPPY upon her return.
We guarantee that for the entire lifetime of the puppy, no matter what happens, we are responsible for its well-being. Life happens, and sometimes people can no longer care for a dog. We will find the dog a new home, no questions asked, and no judgment about the need to return it.
The BUYER agrees that the PUPPY shall not, under any circumstances, be placed in any humane agency, any commercial outlet for sale, given away or sold to a third party without the express written permission of the SELLER.
We never, ever, ever want a puppy to be placed with an animal-rescue group. If the puppy is going to a new home, we want the opportunity to vet that home as well. If you have a family member that adores the dog and wants to adopt it, we will generally be okay with that.
The SELLER has interviewed and screened the BUYER to their satisfaction and has faith that the BUYER will provide a safe, loving, and forever home for the PUPPY. The BUYER agrees to take reasonable care of the PUPPY, and promptly to give or secure the best of attention to the physical welfare of the PUPPY, including appropriate inoculations and medical care. Should the SELLER determine at any time that reasonable care is not being taken and the physical welfare of the PUPPY is in jeopardy, the SELLER then acquires the right to physical possession of the PUPPY and her AKC registration certificate shall be signed over to SELLER as sole owner. Under these circumstances, the BUYER shall be entitled to one hundred ($100.00) dollars less any medical expenses incurred by the condition of the PUPPY upon her return to the SELLER. Following the physical acquisition of the PUPPY by the SELLER, the BUYER has the right to a third-party determination of the suitability of the BUYER’s living conditions and the physical condition of the PUPPY at the time of SELLER’s acquisition. The third party is to be agreed upon by the SELLER and the BUYER and shall be either an accredited veterinarian or an officer of a local dog club.
We have never needed to exercise the “Should the SELLER determine at any time that reasonable care is not being taken and the physical welfare of the PUPPY is in jeopardy” clause. It exists because of situations that other breeders have encountered in the past. The entire purpose is to ensure the well-being of the puppy.

MEDICAL ASSURANCES

The PUPPY is in good health, of sound mind and body and free from communicable diseases, as far as appears to the eye. The BUYER has had the opportunity to have the PUPPY examined by a veterinarian of their choice at their expense and to notify the SELLER of any illness or defect noted. As there have been no reports of any abnormality or deficiency, the SELLER and BUYER agree that the PUPPY was delivered in good health.
The full written vaccination and worming record will be provided to BUYER at the time of delivery.
FANCONI SYNDROME: The SELLER ascertains to have had the PUPPY direct-DNA-tested for Fanconi Syndrome by the University of Missouri-Columbia lab in association with the Orthopedic Foundation for Animals. The result of the Fanconi test is “RESULT”, which indicates that the PUPPY will not be affected by Fanconi Syndrome in later life.
By virtue of our breeding choices, all puppies will be either “clear” or “carriers”; we should never have an “affected” puppy. See OFA's Fanconi Syndrome page for more details.
PROGRESSIVE RETINAL ATROPHY (PRA): The SELLER ascertains to have had the PUPPY direct-DNA-tested for the PRA-BJ1 form of Progressive Retinal Atrophy by the University of Missouri-Columbia lab in association with the Orthopedic Foundation for Animals. The result of the PRA-BJ1 test is “RESULT”, which indicates that the PUPPY will not be affected by PRA-BJ1 in later life.
By virtue of our breeding choices, all puppies should be “normal”; we should never have an “abnormal” puppy. See OFA’s Basenji PRA page for more details.
PERSISTENT PUPILLARY MEMBRANES (PPM): The SELLER ascertains to have had the PUPPY examined by a board certified canine ophthalmologist, VETERINARIAN on DATE OF EXAM. The test results are as follows: left and right eye: “RESULT”. These results enable the PUPPY’s eyes to be certified by Canine Eye Database monitored by the Orthopedic Foundation for Animals (“OFA”) and published in the quarterly report of the Basenji Club of America. A copy of this report will be furnished to the BUYER upon delivery.
By virtue of our breeding choices, all puppies should be “normal”; there may be some small persistent membranes, but these typically disappear by the time the puppy reaches one year of age and do not impact their vision.
The BUYER acknowledges that he has been informed of the potential problems in the breed including FANCONI SYNDROME, PROGRESSIVE RETINAL ATROPHY (PRA), PERSISTENT PUPILLARY MEMBRANES (PPM), and HIP DYSPLASIA (HD). Should the PUPPY become affected with Fanconi Syndrome at any point in the PUPPY’s lifetime, or if PRA or HD develops before the age of ten years and is diagnosed by two completely independently-practicing veterinarians as being affected and the SELLER is still actively breeding Basenjis at the rate of one or more litters every two years, the BUYER is entitled to another puppy of the SELLER’s choice at no charge (shipping not to be included).
We are very, very confident in the test results and our choice of dogs to breed. We have never needed to fulfill the “no-charge puppy” clause, but we absolutely will if we ever need to.

AGREE TO SPAY/NEUTER

The BUYER agrees that the PUPPY shall be spayed after the age of one year or one estrus cycle, whichever comes first, and shall never whelp a litter. After the spay surgery, the BUYER will provide the SELLER with a copy of the PUPPY’s spay certificate (generated by the veterinary clinic) at which time the SELLER will change the AKC registration to reflect that the PUPPY is solely owned by the BUYER.
Historically, breeders would require puppies to be spayed/neutered very, very young. There are developmental reasons why it’s better to wait for the puppy to grow a bit more first.

POTENTIAL BREEDING AND SHOWING

The PUPPY will not be shown. Other than dog shows, the PUPPY is welcome and encouraged to pursue any/all other AKC events and venues such as lure coursing, rally, agility, tracking, obedience, “trick dog”, scentwork/nosework, etc. The SELLER will enthusiastically make introductions and open doors of opportunity for those should the BUYER indicate interest.

TRAINING

The SELLER strongly recommends that the PUPPY be enrolled in a series of positive-reinforcement method group dog training sessions to learn and confirm basic manners including (but not limited to): basic house training, crate training so that the PUPPY is comfortable and clean in a crate in a home or in a car, leash training on a collar (not only on a harness), and the cues “sit”, “stand”, “wait”, “off”, and “come”.

COMMUNICATION

The BUYER agrees to have contact with the SELLER a minimum of twice each calendar year (preferably before July 1st and December 1st) for the lifetime of the PUPPY, even after she is spayed, for the purposes of genetic charting. The rich pedigree information and knowledge that you enjoy with the PUPPY stems from the cooperation of former Taji buyers. Please continue the chain by contributing data for the benefit of future generations.

PUPPY SUPPORT—CUSTOMER SERVICE PROVIDED BY SELLER

The SELLER assumes that the BUYER will need frequent counsel and coaching throughout the first year with the first couple of months being the most intense. The SELLER should always be the BUYER’s first resource and will be available to the BUYER as needed. The value of the SELLER’s time and sharing of their expertise is fully covered by the purchase price and remains available to the BUYER for The PUPPY’s entire lifetime.
We have seen myriad times online where people with Basenjis ask questions which we feel should have been easily answered by their breeder. We always want to be available as your first, best resource for things like this.
