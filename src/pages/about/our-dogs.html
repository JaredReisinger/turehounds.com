What is with all those letters around the dogs’ names?

So glad you asked!

TLDR; hover over the letters to see what title they represent. On mobile, well, you’ll have to keep reading.

In the conformation and performance dog world, we sometimes call this the “alphabet soup”, because it looks like a bunch of random gibberish. In truth, it’s simply the way that AKC and other organizations indicate any titles earned by the dog.

In general, each term in the list is composed of the initial letters that indicate the event, and a final letter which indicates the level of achievement. And note that when a more-advanced title is earned in a given event, it replaces the previous one in the alphabet soup; instead of “CGC CGCA”, you’d just see “CGCA”.

Here’s a quick guide to the ones you might see:

So, you would interpret Disa’s titles (DC / SC CA BCAT CGCA CGCU TKA FDX RATM) as:

  • Dual Champion (both conformation and performance)
  • Senior Courser
  • Coursing Ability
  • Fast CAT—150 points
  • Canine Good Citizen Advanced
  • Canine Good Citizen Urban
  • Trick Dog Advanced
  • Flyball Dog Excellent
  • Barn Hunt Master

... and Spyros’s titles (CH / RN JC CAA BCAT SWN SBA RATO CGCA CGCU TKP) as:

  • Champion (conformation only)
  • Rally Novice
  • Junior Courser
  • Coursing Ability Advanced
  • Fast CAT—150 points
  • Barn Hunt Master
  • Scent Work Novice (includes all four elements: buried, containers, interiors, and exteriors)
  • Scent Work Buried Advanced (supercedes the novice title, but only for buried)
  • Barn Hunt Open
  • Canine Good Citizen Advanced
